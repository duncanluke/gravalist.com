Route Welcome to the Burra 500 Ultra Endurance Gravel Bikepacking Event, starting in Pietermaritzburg, KwaZulu-Natal. This challenging four-day cycling adventure will test your endurance, strength, and mental fortitude as you navigate some of the toughest terrain South Africa has to offer. Starting in June, during the winter season, riders can expect cool temperatures and hilly terrain throughout the route. Learn More Route Highlight Inzinga Ridge Climb The Inzinga Ridge Climb is a challenging cycling segment located in Impendle, South Africa, within the beautiful province of KwaZulu-Natal. This segment covers a distance of 13.97km with a total elevation gain of 610m, making it one of the toughest climbs in the region.The climb begins at an elevation of 1,224m and reaches a peak altitude of 1,800m, with an elevation difference of 576m. The average gradient of the climb is 4.1%, with some sections reaching up to 10%, making it a true test of endurance and strength.The climb takes riders through the scenic Drakensberg Mountains, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and peaks. Along the way, riders will experience the unique flora and fauna of the region, including beautiful wildflowers and grazing wildlife.As riders make their way up the Inzinga Ridge, they will face a series of steep and challenging switchbacks, with each turn bringing them closer to the summit. The climb rewards riders with a sense of achievement and the stunning views of the surrounding landscape.While the Inzinga Ridge Climb is not for the faint of heart, it is a must-ride for any cycling enthusiast visiting the KwaZulu-Natal region. Its challenging gradient and breathtaking views make it a true bucket-list climb for any avid cyclist.So, whether you are an experienced cyclist or simply looking for a challenge, the Inzinga Ridge Climb is the perfect segment to test your limits and experience the beauty of the Drakensberg Mountains.
